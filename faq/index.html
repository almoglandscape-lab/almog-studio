Questions, answered.

Most things people want to know before we begin a garden together.

Who is Almog?
An Israeli landscape artist and outdoor designer. He composes private gardens, rooftops and outdoor spaces with a focus on native Mediterranean planting, stone, water and light.
Where does Almog Studio work?
Across Israel. Mostly private residences, penthouses and small estates. Selected international projects on request.
What kind of gardens?
Honest, site-specific. Drought-wise Mediterranean planting, rooftop gardens, courtyards and contemporary private residences.
How does the design process work?
Six steps: site walk; conversation; written concept; full design; weekly supervision during construction; seasonal care afterwards. More on /process.
How long does it take?
Concept 2–3 weeks. Full design 6–10 weeks. Construction depends on scope. The studio takes a small number of gardens a year.
How much does it cost?
Every garden begins with a paid site visit and concept. Pricing is shared after the first conversation, scaled to scope and site.
Do you work with native plants only?
Native Mediterranean species come first. Naturalised and ornamental plants are used where they earn their place. Drought-wise always.
Do you offer ongoing care?
Yes. Almog stays with most of the gardens he makes, season after season.
What does "Almog" mean?
"Almog" is Hebrew for coral — a living, slow-growing structure. It is also the founder's name.
How do I begin?
Write to hello@almog.studio with a sentence about your site and a photograph. Almog reads everything personally.
