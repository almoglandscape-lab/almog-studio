How a garden is made.

Six steps, from the first walk to a garden that lives. Most of them are listening.

01 — Site walk

A long, unhurried first visit. I walk the site at the time of day you live in it. I listen for wind, sun, neighbours, water, soil. Often I come back twice before saying a word.

02 — Conversation

We sit together — usually in the kitchen — and I ask how the place is actually used. Meals, mornings, play, hosting, rest. The garden begins as a list of small daily moments.

03 — Concept

A short written sketch and one composition drawing. Direction, mood, structure. Approval here protects the budget later — and gives the garden its spine.

04 — Design

Master plan, planting plan, materials palette, lighting. Golden ratio for the bones; native Mediterranean planting for the breath. We choose stone and plants together.

05 — Construction

I supervise the contractors weekly. Stone is laid with hands on the material. Plants are placed by me on site — never by a plan alone. Small changes are made in the field, in real light.

06 — Care

A good garden is never finished — only edited. Season after season, we prune, replace, surprise. Most of my gardens are with me for years.

Budget & time

Projects begin with a paid site visit and concept. Full design typically takes 6–10 weeks; construction depends on scope and contractors. I take a small number of gardens a year — patience is part of the work.
