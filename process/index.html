Process

Five steps, no shortcuts.

A garden is slow on purpose. Here is how one is made, from the first conversation to the seasons of care that follow.

  • 01 · Listening. We begin with you: how you live, where you pause, what the space needs to give back at the end of a day.
  • 02 · Reading the site. Sun, wind, soil, slope and views are mapped before a single line is drawn. The land speaks first.
  • 03 · Composition. The garden is composed by golden-ratio proportion: paths, planting and pause points placed so the space feels calm without saying why.
  • 04 · Planting. Native, climate-fit planting goes in as layers that mature over seasons, not a garden that peaks on day one.
  • 05 · Care. Gardens keep growing. Seasonal editing keeps the place honest, healthy and yours.

How long does it take?

Design moves quickly once we understand the site, usually a few weeks to a clear concept. The garden itself takes longer to become itself, and that's the point. A place worth living in is worth waiting for.
